Mental Illness Representation and Trauma-Based Storytelling Across the Visual Media

Home Page

Why is well-written and responsible representation of mental illness and trauma in the media important?

Mental illness and the impacts of trauma have garnered significant attention in recent years, (particularly over the past decade). These topics are now acknowledged as the formidable forces they truly are: challenging to detect externally and diagnose, yet inflicting profound effects on both the individual experiencing them and those around them. When crafting a narrative involving individuals grappling with serious mental disorders or overcoming/living with trauma, creators/directors have to tread a thin line because, on one side, there lies the risk of trivializing the debilitating nature of these conditions. On the other hand, there's the possibility of portraying them in a manner that can trigger individuals living with these conditions. Trauma-focused storytelling and the depiction of characters navigating mental health struggles can spark controversy in other ways as well. Concerns arise regarding misrepresentation, appropriateness for the intended audience, and the potential romanticization of mental illness and trauma for entertainment purposes. There are also ethical considerations, including the potential for fear-mongering, as seen in films like "Split" (2016) and "Fatal Attraction" (1987), which portray dissociative identity disorder (DID) and borderline personality disorder (BPD) in a very generalized, negative, and aggressive light. Authentic portrayal becomes even more challenging without personal experience of these subjects, close collaboration with individuals who have lived through such experiences, or professionals with firsthand knowledge.

In this exploration of some of my favorite visual media narratives, I will shine a spotlight on some narratives that handle mental illness and trauma in a respectful and appropriate way because responsible portrayal of these themes has the power to validate the experiences of those grappling with mental health challenges/traumatic situations, initiate crucial conversations about these subjects, and contribute to the overall destigmatization and recovery efforts of mental illness. These stories serve as mirrors reflecting our realities, struggles, and triumphs and through their examination, we not only gain insights into fictional worlds and characters, but also deepen our understanding of ourselves.
